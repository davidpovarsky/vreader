## Observations

- The WI-4c-a launch arg works exactly as planned. Pre-WI-4c-a, the
  toggle_present test always XCTSkipped at the section probe because
  the SwiftUI segmented Picker doesn't dispatch tap-to-segment under
  XCUITest. Post-WI-4c-a, the section reaches the toggle row and the
  hittable assertion passes after a small swipe-up nudge (the section
  header was scrolling in at the bottom edge of the panel; the toggle
  row needed an extra swipe to enter the hittable region).
- The slider test's "toggle lookup returns no matches" failure
  appears intermittently — happens on consecutive runs even when the
  toggle_present test passes immediately before. Possible causes:
  iOS 26.5 hierarchy invalidation between test methods, panel state
  leakage across setUp cycles, or a race between `app.launch()` and
  the `--reader-default-layout` UserDefaults write reaching the
  `ReaderSettingsStore.init` path. None of these are WI-4c-a
  regressions — they would have affected the prior XCTSkip path too;
  the prior test just bailed earlier.
- Net assessment: WI-4c-a achieves its purpose (`store.epubLayout`
  pre-seeded to `.paged` so the segmented-Picker tap is no longer in
  the critical path). One of the two Feature31 XCUITest methods now
  passes deterministically; the other needs an XCUITest reliability
  follow-up.
