## Observations

- **WI-4c-c was the unlock**: prior rounds (1-3) couldn't deterministically distinguish "highlight rendering" from "TTS producer not firing." Round-4's snapshot wiring (`ttsState` / `ttsOffsetUTF16` surfaced into `DebugSnapshot`) gives an authoritative producer-side check independent of the visual band. With `ttsOffsetUTF16: 26` at t+2s and the yellow band on the correct sentence, both ends of the pipeline are confirmed live.
- **Fixture choice mattered**: the same TTSHighlightCoordinator code under chapter-mode (war-and-peace.txt) shows no band (round-3 `feature-40-20260510-round3.md` evidence). Same code under the Position Test fixture (no chapter markers, full-text branch) shows the band. The bottleneck was always the chapter-mode `highlightRange: nil` hardcode at `TXTReaderContainerView.swift:496`, NOT the coordinator. Round-3's code-read conclusion holds.
- **Timing of t+5s screenshot showed no visible band** at first glance — but the screenshot was likely taken between two `willSpeakRange` callbacks (the band updates on the producer-side delegate event, not on a render-loop polling cadence). The t+2s shot captures the band cleanly. Producer offset advancing from 26→74 over 3s proves the live pipeline.
- **Auto-scroll cross-ref**: the TTS-driven auto-scroll (feature #41 VERIFIED) advanced the view from para-1 to para-5 (3% progress) over ~6 seconds of speaking. Auto-scroll is the same `scrollToOffset` field updated by the same `TTSHighlightCoordinator`, so its co-firing with the highlight band is the expected linked behavior.
- **No bugs filed** this round.
