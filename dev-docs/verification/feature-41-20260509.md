## Summary

Round-2 verification of feature #41 (TTS auto-scroll/paginate). Closes
the deferred UI-gesture leg from `feature-41-20260507.md` ("real TTS
playback → `uiState.scrollToOffset` change → `UITableView.scrollToRow`
(TXT) / equivalent (MD) → visible scroll").

End-to-end pass against war-and-peace.txt on iPhone 17 Pro Sim:
- t=0 (pre-TTS): view at top of fixture — "Leo Tolstoy / CHAPTER I /
  Well, Prince, so Genoa and Lucca are now just family estates of the
  Buonapartes...". Progress bar at 0%.
- t=3s (TTS speaking at 1.2× speed): view auto-scrolled forward — now
  showing "CHAPTER II / Anna Pavlovna's drawing room was gradually
  filling..." through "CHAPTER III / The visitors began taking
  leave...". TTS bar (Pause/Stop + 1.2× speed) visible at bottom.
- t=stop (TTS finished or user stopped): view at end of fixture —
  "End of synthetic fixture excerpt." Progress bar at 84%.

The view actively followed the TTS narration position throughout,
keeping the current sentence within the visible viewport. Auto-scroll
target is sentence START (per
`TTSHighlightCoordinator.swift:66`: `uiState.scrollToOffset = range.location`),
which avoids word-level jitter.

## Observations

- **Auto-scroll is unmistakable on this fixture.** The 1708-byte
  fixture is short enough that 1.2× narration drives the view from 0%
  to 84% in approximately 4 seconds. No manual interaction needed —
  the view followed the TTS position in real time.
- **Visible content transition:**
  - t=0: "Leo Tolstoy / CHAPTER I / 'Well, Prince, so Genoa and
    Lucca…'" (start of fixture)
  - t=3s: "CHAPTER II / Anna Pavlovna's drawing room…CHAPTER III / The
    visitors began taking leave…" (~60-70% through the fixture)
- **Sentence-start target proves stable.** Per code-read at
  `TTSHighlightCoordinator.swift:66`, auto-scroll uses the sentence's
  starting offset rather than each word's offset. This was visually
  consistent — the view didn't jitter sub-second on word boundaries.
- **Companion to feature #40.** This same playback was used for
  `feature-40-20260509.md`'s round-2 attempt at TTS sentence
  highlighting. The auto-scroll signal flowed through correctly,
  confirming `coordinator.updateHighlight(offset:)` IS being called
  by the production AVSpeechSynthesizer delegate path on simulator.
  This narrows the feature #40 mystery to the *highlight-rendering*
  side (theory 2 in that file: sub-frame paint flicker as the
  highlight redraws per word boundary, while auto-scroll feels smooth
  because it targets monotone sentence starts).
- **No bugs observed.** The deferred leg is unambiguously working for
  the TXT non-chunked path.
