## Summary

Round-2 device verification for feature #8 (Reading position
scrubber/progress bar — Medium priority). Round 1
(`feature-8-20260507.md`, `result: partial`) covered the data
layer with `ScrollProgressHelper` tests (percentage formatting,
chapter-fraction → global-progress math, edge cases) and
production-wiring code-reads. Round 1 explicitly deferred two
UI-gesture slices:

1. End-to-end UI: drag scrubber → position updates → reader scrolls.
2. End-to-end UI: progress bar updates live as user scrolls reader.

This round runs both slices end-to-end on iPhone 17 Pro Sim
(iOS 26.4) at v3.14.90 with CU drag/swipe gestures.

**Result: pass.** Both directions verified visually with
percentage labels matching the visible scroll position. Status
moves from `DONE` to **`VERIFIED`**.

## CU-driven repro on iPhone 17 Pro Sim (iOS 26.4) at v3.14.90

1. `xcrun simctl install "iPhone 17 Pro" <Debug-iphonesimulator>/vreader.app`
2. `xcrun simctl terminate "iPhone 17 Pro" com.vreader.app`
3. `xcrun simctl launch "iPhone 17 Pro" com.vreader.app --uitesting --seed-position-test --reset-preferences`
4. Open Position Test Book → reader (TXT, 100 paragraphs).

### Forward direction: scrubber drag → reader scrolls

5. **Baseline state**: reader at top, "Paragraph 1: ..." visible. Progress label "0%". Scrubber thumb at left end.
   - Captured at `feature-8-r2-baseline-0pct-20260508.png`.
6. **Drag scrubber thumb from left (x≈275, y=580) to right (x≈420, y=580)**.
7. **Observed**: reader content jumps to "Paragraph 91: ..." (and 92, 93, 94 below). Both progress labels (chapter % at center-bottom, book % at bottom-left) update to **"90%"**. Scrubber thumb moves to ~90% along the track.
   - Captured at `feature-8-r2-after-scrubber-drag-90pct-20260508.png`.

### Reverse direction: content scroll → progress bar updates

8. **Drag-up gesture on reader content** (start y=300, end y=550 — swipe-down to reveal earlier content).
9. **Observed**: reader content moves back, paragraphs **88, 89, 90** now visible. Both progress labels update to **"85%"**. Scrubber thumb position reflects the new scroll location. Bottom-right shows reading-time **"5m read"**.
   - Captured at `feature-8-r2-after-content-scroll-85pct-20260508.png`.

The bidirectional sync (scrubber ↔ scroll position) works live —
no observable latency between drag/scroll and label/thumb update.

## Observations

- **Bidirectional sync is live** — both the scrubber-drag → scroll
  direction and the content-scroll → scrubber-update direction
  work without observable latency. The labels (top center "%" and
  bottom-left "%") update in step.
- **Chapter-aware percentage**: the position-test fixture has a
  synthetic 1-chapter index covering the entire document
  (`globalStartUTF16 = 0, textLengthUTF16 = total`), so
  chapter-fraction equals book-fraction in this round. For
  multi-chapter books the labels would diverge — chapter-relative
  vs book-global progress — covered by round-1's
  `ChapterProgressCalculator.globalFraction` test contract.
- **CU `scroll` action did NOT reach the UITextView** initially
  (zero-effect call); switched to `left_click_drag` which produced
  a swipe gesture that the textview's pan recognizer accepted.
  Documenting as a CU+UITextView interaction note for future
  iterations: prefer `left_click_drag` over `scroll` for
  programmatic UITextView scroll.
- **Reading-time tracker observed working** — "5m read" label at
  bottom-right reflects accumulated session time. Not a feature #8
  acceptance criterion directly, but cross-cuts with reading-stats
  features.
- **Chrome stayed visible throughout** — no need to tap content
  to toggle chrome; the chrome auto-shows when reader is opened
  and persists during programmatic interaction (per the
  `isChromeVisible` state machine in
  `TXTReaderContainerView`).
- **Result: `pass`** — both deferred UI-gesture slices from round
  1 confirmed end-to-end. Status moves to `VERIFIED`.
