# Feature #85 ‚Äî Seamless Readium EPUB cross-chapter scroll ‚Äî Gate-5b acceptance

Approach C (hybrid re-route): scroll-mode EPUBs route to the legacy #71
`EPUBContinuousScrollCoordinator` single-column stitch (seamless), while Readium
stays the paged engine. Position is carried across the engine swap by
`ReaderPositionHandoff` + `EPUBScrollAnchorResolver`.

**Result: `partial`.** The headline criterion (seam removal) and the riskiest new
logic (cross-engine position restore) are device-verified PASS at v3.54.0. Three
residual criteria are genuinely blocked CU-free on this harness and are covered by
Gate-3 unit tests (44) + the Gate-4 audit (ship-as-is, Codex `019e95b8`/`019e95d9`,
v3 after 2 reverts that caught real position-loss bugs). Per the SCHEMA's strict
`pass` semantics, any non-device-verified criterion makes the run `partial`, so
**#85 stays DONE** (not flipped to VERIFIED) pending the residual legs.

## Observations

- **The seam is genuinely gone.** Both chapters live in ONE continuous scroll
  container (one DIV, scrollHeight 13881px) ‚Äî there is no per-resource iframe
  boundary the way Readium's paged-scroll has. "Paragraph 40 of ALPHA" flows
  straight into "CHAPTER TWO ‚Äî BRAVO" with only the normal inter-chapter heading.
- **The cross-engine position restore is the strongest evidence.** Paging Readium
  to CHARLIE (chapter 3) and toggling to scroll landed the legacy stitch with a
  ¬±1 window *centered on CHARLIE* ‚Äî exactly what `ReaderPositionHandoff` +
  `EPUBScrollAnchorResolver` (container‚ÜîOPF href tolerance) are supposed to do.
  This is the half of the handoff that READS the position; it is device-verified.
- **Harness boundaries (next time, don't re-discover):**
  - Programmatic `document.scrollingElement.scrollTop` reports `max:64` ‚Äî the real
    scroller is a nested DIV; query `div[scrollHeight>5000]`, not the document.
  - Synthetic scroll (idb swipe, even `--duration 0.6`) does NOT drive the legacy
    WKWebView inner scroll, and programmatic scrollTop does NOT fire the
    rAF-throttled `onWindowedPosition` observer ‚Üí the legacy-side handoff RECORD
    can't be populated CU-free. The Readium-side record (page-turn navigator
    events) DOES fire, which is why paged‚Üíscroll is verifiable but scroll‚Üípaged
    record is not.
  - idb long-press did not engage Readium WKWebView text selection on 2 attempts;
    highlight-CREATE-via-selection stays harness-blocked here (feature #42 logged
    the same class of gap).
- **Footer chapter indicator lag** after a programmatic scroll is the rAF-observer
  gap, not a product bug ‚Äî on a real-mount restore (criterion 4) the indicator
  showed "Chapter 3 of 4" correctly because it's computed from the restored
  position, not from the rAF scroll callback.
