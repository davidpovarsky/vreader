## "Try harder" investigation (2026-06-06) — what was attempted and found

The user directed an autonomous attempt to verify residuals 5/6 before accepting the
exception. Results:

- **The prior "rAF is paused on the virtual display" claim is WRONG.** A direct probe
  (a `WKWebView` hosting a self-perpetuating `requestAnimationFrame` loop in a
  key+visible window) ran many frames (`window.__n > 1`) — **rAF fires on this host**.
  The prior session's failure came from *synthetic* `scrollTop=` on the wrong element
  (the document, `max:64`) not firing a real scroll event, conflated with an rAF pause.
- **The real residual-1 blocker (gesture-routing).** The #71 legacy continuous scroll
  scrolls a **nested DIV** (`scrollHeight > 5000`), not the WKWebView's outer
  scrollview. A purpose-built XCUITest (`--seed-multi-chapter-epub` launch seed in
  scroll layout) opened the book and read the bottom-chrome reading-% scrubber, then
  drove `app.swipeUp()` ×16 — the recorded reading-% stayed **0% → 0%**. XCUITest HID
  swipes (like the prior idb gestures) do not drive the nested-DIV scroll.
- **The read-back gap.** Even when the scroll is driven programmatically (which fires
  the real scroll event → rAF `report` → `onWindowedPosition`, since rAF fires), the
  recorded EPUB continuous-scroll position is **not exposed CU-free**: `snapshot.position`
  is cfi-nil for continuous EPUB, and `readingProgress` is not in the DebugSnapshot schema.
- **Conclusion**: residual 5's RECORD path is not globally rAF-blocked, but its CU-free
  *device* verification on this host is blocked by (a) the nested-DIV not being driven by
  XCUITest/idb gestures and (b) the recorded position not being CU-free readable. A real
  physical-display device (real finger-scroll drives the nested DIV) or an idb build
  configured to drive the inner scroll would close it; the RECORD logic itself is
  unit-tested + audited. The throwaway probe/test/seed-wiring were reverted (not committed).
